## The 5 Workflows

### Competitive Intelligence Pack

**Use when:**
- Quarterly competitive reviews
- Preparing pitch decks or board presentations
- Updating positioning strategy
- Planning feature roadmap (what are competitors building?)
- Market entry analysis (who's already here?)

**Don't use when:**
- You only care about 1-2 competitors (just research them manually)
- You need real-time competitive tracking (this is a snapshot)
- Competitors are private with no public info (analysis will be thin)

**You'll get:**
- Competitive landscape map (2×2 positioning)
- 5 competitor profiles (product, pricing, positioning, reviews, tech)
- Feature comparison matrix
- Pricing comparison
- SWOT analysis for each competitor
- Strategic recommendations (positioning opportunities, feature gaps, pricing moves)

**Time saved:** 4 hrs → 30 min (8× faster)

**Example scenario:**
> "I'm preparing a board deck and need to show our competitive landscape. I want to analyze Monday.com, Teamwork, Float, Asana, and ClickUp to understand where we stand on features and pricing."

**Framework:** DHM Model (Gibson Biddle), April Dunford Positioning

---

### Batch Interview Synthesis

**Use when:**
- You have 5-10+ customer interview transcripts
- You need to extract Jobs-to-be-Done and opportunities
- You're building personas from research data
- You want to create an opportunity solution tree (Teresa Torres)
- Quarterly research synthesis (consolidate all interviews)

**Don't use when:**
- You have <3 interviews (do it manually — too little data)
- Interviews aren't customer-focused (e.g., internal stakeholder interviews)
- You need real-time analysis during a live interview (this is post-interview)

**You'll get:**
- Individual interview snapshots (JTBD, opportunities, quotes, observations)
- Research synthesis (top themes, patterns, contradictions)
- Data-backed personas (grounded in actual research)
- Opportunity solution tree (prioritized opportunities with evidence)

**Time saved:** 3 hrs → 20 min (9× faster)

**Example scenario:**
> "I just finished 8 user interviews about our onboarding flow. I need to extract the key pain points, create personas, and build an opportunity tree for our Q2 roadmap."

**Framework:** Teresa Torres Continuous Discovery, Jobs-to-be-Done, Atomic Research

---

### Hypothesis Tester

**Use when:**
- Metrics debugging ("Why did conversion drop 20%?")
- Root cause analysis ("Why is churn increasing?")
- Pre-mortems ("What could cause this launch to fail?")
- Problem diagnosis ("Why aren't users adopting this feature?")
- A/B test analysis ("Why did variant B lose?")

**Don't use when:**
- You already know the root cause (just fix it)
- You have no data to investigate hypotheses (you'll get weak conclusions)
- The problem is simple/obvious (don't overcomplicate)

**You'll get:**
- 5 competing hypotheses investigated in parallel
- Supporting AND disconfirming evidence for each
- Adversarial debate summary (teammates challenge each other)
- Most likely cause with confidence % (honest about uncertainty)
- Recommended validation tests (how to confirm/disprove)
- Next steps if validated or not

**Time saved:** 2 hrs → 15 min (8× faster)

**Example scenario:**
> "Our mobile conversion dropped 20% last week. I don't know if it's a bug, UX issue, traffic quality problem, or something else. I need to investigate multiple theories quickly."

**Framework:** Scientific Method, Pre-Mortems (Shreyas Doshi), Assumption Testing

---

### Market Sizing Analyzer

**Use when:**
- Fundraising pitch decks (TAM slide)
- Strategy planning / OKR setting (is this market big enough?)
- Market entry decisions (should we enter this segment?)
- Board presentations (market context and opportunity)
- Business case development (revenue potential sizing)

**Don't use when:**
- Your market is too niche to have public data (analysis will be rough)
- You need exact precision (market sizing is always estimates)
- You already have analyst reports (just use those)

**You'll get:**
- TAM/SAM/SOM estimates (low/mid/high ranges)
- 5 methodologies compared:
  - Analyst Reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
  - Competitor Financials (public company extrapolation)
  - Bottom-Up Sizing (# customers × ARPU)
  - Top-Down Sizing (industry size × % addressable)
  - Adjacent Market Proxies (similar markets)
- Triangulated estimate (weighted average)
- Assumptions & sensitivity analysis
- Recommended use (which estimate for fundraising vs. planning vs. board)

**Time saved:** 4 hrs → 45 min (5× faster)

**Example scenario:**
> "I'm raising a Series A and need a credible TAM estimate for my pitch deck. I want to triangulate across analyst reports, bottom-up sizing, and competitor data to show it's a venture-scale market."

**Framework:** TAM/SAM/SOM Analysis, Bottom-Up + Top-Down Sizing

---

### Voice of Customer Analysis

**Use when:**
- Quarterly roadmap planning (what should we build next?)
- Feature prioritization (which pain points matter most?)
- Product-market fit assessment (are we solving the right problems?)
- Customer retention analysis (why are customers churning?)
- Support ticket trend analysis (what's driving volume?)

**Don't use when:**
- You have only 1 VoC source (need 2+ for cross-source validation)
- Your feedback is too sparse (<20 items per source)
- You need real-time VoC tracking (this is a snapshot)

**You'll get:**
- Top 3 cross-source themes (validated pain points)
- Prioritized pain points (frequency × urgency matrix with P0/P1/P2)
- Source-specific insights (support tickets, NPS, sales calls, community, reviews)
- Feature requests aggregated across sources
- Sentiment analysis (overall + by source)
- Opportunity areas:
  - Quick wins (< 1 sprint)
  - Strategic bets (> 1 quarter)
- Recommended next steps (immediate / short-term / long-term)

**Time saved:** 3 hrs → 30 min (6× faster)

**Example scenario:**
> "I have 500 support tickets, 200 NPS comments, 10 sales call notes, and 50 community posts from last quarter. I need to synthesize all this feedback to plan our Q2 roadmap and identify quick wins."

**Framework:** VoC Synthesis, Theme Extraction, Atomic Research

## Workflow Comparison

| Workflow | Best For | Time Saved | Input Required | Output Type |
|----------|----------|------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Competitive Intelligence** | Understanding market landscape | 4 hrs → 30 min | 3-5 competitor names | Strategic report |
| **Batch Interview Synthesis** | Extracting insights from research | 3 hrs → 20 min | 5-10 interview transcripts | Research artifacts |
| **Hypothesis Tester** | Debugging problems/metrics | 2 hrs → 15 min | Problem statement | Root cause analysis |
| **Market Sizing** | Fundraising, strategy | 4 hrs → 45 min | Market definition | TAM/SAM/SOM estimates |
| **Voice of Customer** | Roadmap planning, prioritization | 3 hrs → 30 min | 2+ VoC sources | Prioritized pain points |

## When NOT to Use Workflows

**Use individual skills instead when:**
- You need a single output (PRD, roadmap, competitive profile)
- The task is straightforward (no research synthesis needed)
- You're exploring/drafting (workflows are for production-ready outputs)

**Do manual work when:**
- You have <3 data points (too little to synthesize)
- You need real human judgment on sensitive topics
- The output needs deep domain expertise (workflows give you 80%, you refine the 20%)

## Workflow Selection Examples

### Scenario 1: "Our conversion is down and I don't know why"
**Use:** Hypothesis Tester
**Why:** You need to investigate multiple theories quickly, test each with evidence, find most likely cause
**Alternative:** If you already have a theory, just investigate it directly (don't overcomplicate)

---

### Scenario 2: "I need to plan Q2 roadmap"
**Use:** Voice of Customer Analysis + Batch Interview Synthesis
**Why:** VoC gives you prioritized pain points, interviews give you deep JTBD understanding
**Alternative:** If you have clear product vision and don't need validation, skip workflows and just write roadmap

---

### Scenario 3: "I'm fundraising and need a TAM estimate"
**Use:** Market Sizing Analyzer
**Why:** Triangulates 5 methodologies, gives you defensible estimate with sources
**Alternative:** If you have recent analyst report (Gartner, Forrester), just cite that

---

### Scenario 4: "I want to understand our competitive position"
**Use:** Competitive Intelligence Pack
**Why:** Analyzes 5 competitors in parallel, gives you positioning map and strategic recommendations
**Alternative:** If you only care about 1-2 competitors, manually research them (faster)

---

### Scenario 5: "I have 10 customer interviews and need personas"
**Use:** Batch Interview Synthesis
**Why:** Extracts JTBD, creates data-backed personas, builds opportunity tree
**Alternative:** If you have <3 interviews, manually synthesize (not enough data for workflow)

## Getting Started

**First time using workflows?**

1. **Start with Competitive Intelligence** — Easiest to test, clear inputs, immediate value
2. **Then try Hypothesis Tester** — Great for metrics debugging (you'll use this often)
3. **Finally Batch Interview Synthesis or VoC** — Once you have research data

**Best practices:**
- ✅ Read the workflow README first (understand what you'll get)
- ✅ Update your context files (`/context/company.md`, `/context/product.md`) before running
- ✅ Have inputs ready (competitor names, interview transcripts, VoC sources)
- ✅ Don't interrupt workflows — let agent teams finish (20-45 min)
- ✅ Validate outputs — workflows give you 80%, you refine the 20%

---

## Troubleshooting

**"The workflow output is generic"**
→ Update your `/context/` files (company.md, product.md, personas.md) — workflows use this for personalization

**"I'm not sure which workflow to use"**
→ Start at the top of this guide with the decision tree, or ask: "What's my goal?" (understand market vs. synthesize research vs. debug problem)

**"The workflow took longer than expected"**
→ Workflows with more inputs (10 interviews, 5 competitors) take longer. Estimates are for standard inputs (5 competitors, 5-8 interviews).

**"I don't have the right inputs"**
→ Check the workflow README for "Input Required" section. If you don't have the inputs, either gather them first or use a different workflow.

**"The output didn't answer my question"**
→ Workflows are designed for common PM scenarios. If your question is unique, use individual skills or manual work instead.

## Feedback & Iteration

**After using a workflow:**
1. Grade the output (A/B/C)
2. Note what was missing or wrong
3. Improve your inputs (better context files, more data)
4. Re-run if needed (workflows are fast enough to iterate)

**Workflows improve with:**
- Better context files (personalized outputs)
- More input data (richer analysis)
- Clearer problem statements (targeted insights)
