# Competitive Landscape

---

## Direct Competitors

### Flatiron School

**Website:** https://flatironschool.com
**What they do:** Immersive on-campus and online programs in software engineering, data science, cybersecurity, and AI
**Target Audience:** Career changers and aspiring tech professionals
**Pricing Model:** Tuition-based (~$16,900); financing options available

**Our Assessment:**
- **Their strength:** Strong brand recognition, robust career services with dedicated 1:1 coaching, broad program offerings including AI
- **Their weakness:** Significantly higher price point; less flexible format options
- **Why we win:** More affordable, lifetime career support (vs. limited post-grad window), Python-first curriculum depth

---

### Codesmith

**Website:** https://www.codesmith.io
**What they do:** Full-time (13-week) and part-time (38-week) remote software engineering immersive programs, plus NYC on-site
**Target Audience:** Aspiring software engineers seeking senior-level roles
**Pricing Model:** Tuition-based (~$19,950); ISA and financing available

**Our Assessment:**
- **Their strength:** Reputation for producing senior-level engineers; deep JavaScript/CS focus; strong alumni network
- **Their weakness:** Very expensive; JavaScript-only (no data, cyber, QA tracks); highly selective admissions
- **Why we win:** Multiple career tracks (not just SE), more accessible pricing and admissions, Python-first approach

---

### devCodeCamp

**Website:** https://devcodecamp.com
**What they do:** Online bootcamp offering software engineering, web development, and data analytics
**Target Audience:** Career changers seeking online, flexible bootcamp training
**Pricing Model:** Tuition-based; similar price range

**Our Assessment:**
- **Their strength:** Similar flexible online format; competitive pricing; overlapping program offerings
- **Their weakness:** Smaller brand presence; fewer program tracks (no cybersecurity/QA)
- **Why we win:** Broader program portfolio (cyber, QA), job guarantee, lifetime career support, stronger reviews

---

### Nucamp

**Website:** https://www.nucamp.co
**What they do:** Budget-friendly coding bootcamps with community-focused learning
**Target Audience:** Budget-conscious career changers and beginners
**Pricing Model:** Low-cost tuition (~$458–$2,604); most affordable option in market

**Our Assessment:**
- **Their strength:** Extremely affordable; low barrier to entry; good for absolute beginners
- **Their weakness:** Less intensive; outcomes data less impressive; limited career services
- **Why we win:** More comprehensive programs, stronger job placement support, job guarantee, deeper curriculum

---

## Market Position

Coding Temple sits in the **mid-market sweet spot**: more affordable than premium bootcamps (Flatiron, Codesmith) but more comprehensive and career-focused than budget options (Nucamp). Key differentiators are the job guarantee, lifetime career support, and Python-first multi-track curriculum.
