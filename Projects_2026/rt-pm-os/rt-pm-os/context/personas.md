## Persona 1: The Career Changer

**Who they are:** Non-tech professional (25-40) in a field they've outgrown — retail management, administrative roles, customer service, or other non-technical careers. No CS degree.

**Goals:**
- Break into a tech career with a livable salary
- Gain practical, job-ready skills quickly (not a 4-year degree)
- Get hired at a reputable company within months of graduating

**Frustrations:**
- Feels stuck in a career with limited growth or earning potential
- Overwhelmed by the number of bootcamp options and unsure who to trust
- Worried about investing $5K–$14K without a guaranteed outcome
- Self-teaching hasn't worked — needs structure and accountability

**Jobs-to-Be-Done:**
- "When I realize my current career has hit a ceiling, I need to gain marketable tech skills quickly, so I can transition into a higher-paying, more fulfilling career"
- "When I'm evaluating bootcamps, I need confidence that I'll actually get hired, so I can justify the financial and time investment"

## Persona 2: The Recent Graduate

**Who they are:** Recent college graduate (21-26) with a non-technical or general degree who needs practical skills the job market demands. May have some exposure to tech but no deep technical training.

**Goals:**
- Land a first tech job quickly after graduation
- Build a portfolio of real projects to show employers
- Get career guidance and job search support

**Frustrations:**
- College degree didn't prepare them for technical roles
- Entry-level jobs require skills they don't have
- Competing against candidates with CS degrees or bootcamp certificates
- Uncertain which tech path (SE, data, cyber) is right for them

**Jobs-to-Be-Done:**
- "When I can't find a job with my current degree, I need to add technical skills to my resume, so I can compete for roles in the tech industry"
- "When I'm unsure which tech career to pursue, I need guidance on which path fits my strengths, so I can invest my time wisely"

---

## Persona 3: The Upskiller

**Who they are:** Working professional (28-45) who wants to add technical skills to complement their current career — a marketing manager learning data analytics, a project manager learning software basics, or a professional pivoting within tech.

**Goals:**
- Add data, cybersecurity, or development skills to current role
- Advance in current career or pivot to a more technical position
- Learn on a flexible schedule that works around a full-time job

**Frustrations:**
- Can't quit their job to attend a full-time program
- Needs flexible, self-paced options that fit around work
- Previous attempts at online courses lacked structure and support
- Needs skills that apply immediately to their current work

**Jobs-to-Be-Done:**
- "When my role increasingly requires technical skills I don't have, I need to learn data analytics or cybersecurity on a flexible schedule, so I can stay relevant and advance in my career"
- "When I want to transition into a more technical role, I need a structured program with career support, so I can make the switch without a gap in employment"
