# Competitive Intelligence Pack

**5 competitors. 30 minutes. Complete intel.**

---

## What This Workflow Does

Analyzes 5 competitors in parallel using agent teams. Each AI teammate researches one competitor (product, pricing, positioning, reviews, tech stack), then synthesizes a comprehensive competitive intelligence report with strategic recommendations.

**Time saved:** 4 hours → 30 minutes (8× faster)

---

## When to Use This

- Quarterly competitive reviews
- Market entry analysis (new market, new segment)
- Pitch deck preparation (investor/board presentations)
- Positioning strategy (differentiation, messaging)
- Feature gap analysis (what to build vs. competitors)
- Pricing strategy updates

---

## What You'll Get

**Single comprehensive report** with:

1. **Executive Summary** — Top 3-5 findings (threats, opportunities, gaps)
2. **Competitive Landscape Map** — 2×2 positioning (where you stand vs. competitors)
3. **Individual Competitor Profiles** — Deep dive on each competitor:
   - Product features & positioning
   - Pricing tiers & value metrics
   - Strengths & weaknesses
   - DHM analysis (Delight, Hard-to-copy, Margin-enhancing)
   - Customer sentiment (G2 ratings, top praise/complaints)
   - Threat level assessment
4. **Feature Comparison Matrix** — Side-by-side capabilities
5. **Pricing Comparison** — Tiers, value metrics, positioning
6. **SWOT Analysis** — For each competitor
7. **Strategic Recommendations** — Actionable insights:
   - Positioning opportunities
   - Feature development priorities
   - Pricing strategy
   - Marketing angles
   - Threat mitigation

---

## How It Works

### 1. You Provide
- 3-5 competitor names
- (Optional) Analysis focus (pricing, features, positioning, etc.)

### 2. Agent Team Spawns
- 5 AI teammates work in parallel (1 per competitor)
- Each researches: product, pricing, positioning, reviews, tech stack
- Lead AI synthesizes findings into comprehensive report

### 3. You Receive
- Competitive intelligence report (~20-30 minutes)
- Strategic recommendations based on your context
- All sources cited (websites, G2, pricing pages)

---

## Framework

**Gibson Biddle's DHM Model:**
- **Delight:** What delights competitors' customers?
- **Hard-to-copy:** What's their competitive moat?
- **Margin-enhancing:** What gives them pricing power?

**April Dunford's Positioning Framework:**
- Competitive alternatives
- Unique attributes
- Customer value
- Target market fit

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be specific** — Use exact competitor names or URLs
2. **Update context first** — Strategic recommendations are better when we know your product
3. **Focus if needed** — If you only care about pricing, say so upfront
4. **Validate pricing** — Public pricing changes frequently; double-check critical numbers
5. **Share strategically** — Pull relevant sections for team discussions (don't share 20-page doc)

## Limitations

**Can't do:**
- ❌ Access private/internal competitor data

**Does:**
- ✅ Synthesize public competitive intel in 30 minutes
